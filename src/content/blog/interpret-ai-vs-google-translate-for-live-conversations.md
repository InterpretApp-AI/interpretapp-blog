Google Translate is the first tool most people think of for translation. But when you need to translate a **live conversation** — a meeting, a lecture, a church service — the requirements are completely different from pasting text into a box.

## The Core Difference

**Google Translate** translates text you type or paste. It has a conversation mode on mobile, but it requires you to tap a button each time someone speaks, and it works for one language pair at a time.

**Interpret AI** captures live audio continuously, transcribes it in real time with **automatic speaker differentiation**, and translates into any of 70+ languages simultaneously. No tapping, no pausing, no switching.

## Feature Comparison

| Feature | Interpret AI | Google Translate |
|---|---|---|
| Live continuous transcription | Yes | No (tap-to-speak only) |
| Speaker identification | Yes, automatic | No |
| Simultaneous multi-language translation | Yes (70+ at once) | One pair at a time |
| Shareable broadcast link | Yes | No |
| Live translated audio | Yes | Limited (conversation mode) |
| Transcript saved & searchable | Yes | No |
| Word-level timestamps | Yes | No |
| Audio playback with transcript sync | Yes | No |

## When Google Translate Is Fine

- Translating a menu, sign, or short text
- Quick one-off phrase lookups
- Reading a foreign-language webpage

## When You Need Interpret AI

- **Live meetings** where multiple people speak and you need a running transcript with speaker labels
- **Lectures or presentations** where an audience needs real-time translations in multiple languages
- **Church services** or events with a multilingual audience
- **Any situation** where you need to go back and review what was said — with audio playback synced to the transcript

## The Transcript Editor Advantage

After a conversation ends, Google Translate gives you nothing. Interpret AI saves the full transcript with **word-level timestamps**. Open it in the transcript editor and click any word to hear exactly how it was said. Edit, translate into more languages, or share it.

## Desktop App

Interpret AI also has a **standalone desktop app** for Mac and Windows. It captures audio from any application on your computer — not just your browser. This means you can transcribe and translate audio from any source: video calls, media players, webinars, or local recordings.
