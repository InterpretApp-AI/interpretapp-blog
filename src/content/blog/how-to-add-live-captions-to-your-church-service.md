Every congregation includes members who are deaf, hard of hearing, or speak a different language at home. Live captions make sure nobody misses the message.

## Why Live Captions Matter for Churches

- **Hearing accessibility** — members with hearing loss can follow along in real time
- **Multilingual congregations** — translate the sermon into Spanish, Korean, Tagalog, or any of 70+ languages simultaneously
- **Visitors and newcomers** — captions lower the barrier for people who aren't yet comfortable with the primary language
- **Parents in the lobby** — follow the service from anywhere with a phone

## How to Set It Up with Interpret AI

### 1. Create an Account

Go to [interpretapp.ai](https://interpretapp.ai) and sign up. You get 100 free credits to try it out before committing.

### 2. Start a Broadcast Session

From the **Transcribe** page, click to start a session. Interpret AI uses **real-time speaker differentiation** — so when the pastor speaks, then a worship leader, then someone reads scripture, each speaker is identified and labeled automatically.

### 3. Share the Broadcast Link

Share the broadcast link with your congregation. Members open it on their phones and select their preferred language. The captions update live as the service happens.

### 4. Enable Live Audio (Optional)

Members can toggle **Live Audio** to hear a translated version of the sermon spoken by AI — perfect for congregants who are more comfortable listening than reading.

## After the Service

Every session is saved automatically. You can:

- Share the full transcript with members who missed the service
- Review the transcript in the **editor with audio playback** — click any word to jump to that exact moment in the recording, thanks to **word-level timestamps**
- Translate the saved transcript into additional languages for your church bulletin or website
