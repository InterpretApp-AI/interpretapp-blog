A one-hour meeting generates thousands of words. Finding what you need in that wall of text takes time. The Interpret AI chatbot lets you **ask questions about your transcript** and get instant, context-aware answers.

## How It Works

After a transcription session (live or from an uploaded file), open the **AI chatbot**. It has full context of the transcript — every word, every speaker, every timestamp. Just ask what you need.

## What You Can Ask

### Summaries
- "Summarize this meeting in 3 bullet points"
- "What were the main topics discussed?"
- "Give me a one-paragraph summary for an email"

### Decisions and Action Items
- "What decisions were made?"
- "List all action items and who's responsible"
- "What deadlines were mentioned?"

### Specific Information
- "What did Sarah say about the Q3 budget?"
- "When was the product launch date mentioned?"
- "What were the concerns raised about the new feature?"

### Analysis
- "What questions were left unanswered?"
- "Were there any disagreements? About what?"
- "What follow-up meetings were scheduled?"

## Suggested Prompts

Don't know what to ask? The chatbot provides **suggested prompts** based on the content of your transcript. It analyzes what was discussed and offers relevant questions you might want answered.

## Works with Speaker Context

Because Interpret AI uses **real-time speaker differentiation**, the chatbot knows who said what. You can ask speaker-specific questions:

- "Summarize everything the client said"
- "What questions did the interviewer ask?"
- "What was the CEO's main point?"

## Verify with Audio

When the chatbot surfaces an important quote or decision, you can verify it instantly. Open the **transcript editor**, find the passage, and click any word to hear the original audio with **word-level timestamps**. Trust but verify.

## Live and After the Fact

The chatbot works both during a live session and after. During a meeting, ask it to "summarize what's been discussed so far." After the meeting, use it to generate follow-up emails, extract action items, or find specific information without re-reading everything.

## Use Cases

- **Executives** who attend back-to-back meetings and need quick summaries
- **Students** who want to extract key concepts from a lecture
- **Researchers** analyzing interview transcripts for themes
- **Legal professionals** searching depositions for specific statements
- **Anyone** who records conversations and doesn't want to re-listen to the whole thing
