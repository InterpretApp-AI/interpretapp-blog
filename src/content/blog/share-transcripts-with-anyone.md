After a meeting, lecture, or event, you often need to share what was said with people who weren't there. Interpret AI makes this simple with **public transcript sharing**.

## How Sharing Works

1. Open any saved transcript in your **Documents**
2. Click **Share** to generate a public link
3. Send the link to anyone — they can read the full transcript without creating an account
4. Optionally include the **audio recording** so recipients can listen along

That's it. No invitations, no accounts, no access requests.

## What Recipients See

When someone opens your shared link, they get a **read-only view** of the transcript with:

- Full text with **speaker labels** identifying who said what
- **Timestamps** showing when each segment was spoken
- **Audio playback** (if you chose to include it) — synced to the transcript with word-level precision
- Clean, readable formatting — no app chrome or login prompts

## Control Who Sees What

- **Revoke access anytime** — click "Stop Sharing" and the link stops working immediately
- **Share selectively** — each transcript has its own link, so you control exactly what's shared
- **Private by default** — nothing is shared until you explicitly generate a link

## Use Cases

### After a Meeting
Share the transcript with team members who couldn't attend. They get the full conversation with speaker labels — better than secondhand meeting notes.

### Client Deliverables
Consultants and agencies can share polished transcripts with clients as part of their deliverables. Include audio so clients can verify key moments.

### Academic Collaboration
Students and researchers can share lecture transcripts with classmates or collaborators. The **word-level timestamps** let recipients click any passage to hear the original audio.

### Event Follow-Up
After a conference talk, workshop, or church service, share the transcript with attendees. They can review it in their own time, or **translate it** into their preferred language.

## Translated Sharing

Before sharing, you can translate the transcript into any of **70+ languages**. The shared link includes the translation alongside the original — useful for international teams or multilingual audiences.
