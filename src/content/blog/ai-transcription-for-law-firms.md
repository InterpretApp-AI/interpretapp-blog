Legal work lives and dies by the record. Depositions, client calls, hearings, mediations — if it wasn't documented, it didn't happen. AI transcription makes documentation faster, cheaper, and more accurate.

## Why Law Firms Are Switching to AI Transcription

Traditional transcription services are slow (24-48 hour turnaround), expensive ($1-3 per minute), and still require human review. AI transcription gives you a **real-time transcript** as the conversation happens — and it costs a fraction of the price.

## How Interpret AI Works for Legal

### Real-Time Transcription

Start a session during a client call, deposition, or meeting. The transcript appears in real time with **automatic speaker differentiation** — each attorney, witness, and party is identified and labeled separately.

### Word-Level Timestamps

Every word is tied to its exact moment in the audio. This is critical for legal work:

- **Cite specific testimony** by clicking the word and hearing exactly how it was said
- **Verify accuracy** by playing back the audio synced to the transcript
- **Reference timestamps** when cross-referencing with video depositions

### Audio Playback in the Editor

Open any saved transcript and play back the recording. The editor highlights words as they're spoken. Click any word to jump to that timestamp. This makes reviewing and correcting transcripts dramatically faster than scrubbing through audio manually.

### Speaker Management

After transcription, rename speakers from generic labels ("Speaker 1", "Speaker 2") to actual names. The labels update throughout the entire transcript.
