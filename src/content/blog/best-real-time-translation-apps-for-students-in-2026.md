If you're an international student sitting in a lecture delivered in a language you're still learning, you need translation that happens **right now** — not after class when you're reviewing notes.

## What Students Actually Need

- **Continuous live translation** — not tap-to-translate, but always-on transcription during a full lecture
- **Speaker identification** — know whether the professor, a TA, or a classmate is speaking
- **Saved transcripts** — go back and review what was said, with audio
- **Works on a phone** — you shouldn't need extra hardware in the lecture hall

## How Interpret AI Works for Students

### During the Lecture

Open [interpretapp.ai](https://interpretapp.ai) on your laptop or phone and start a session. The app transcribes the lecture in real time and translates it into your preferred language. **Real-time speaker differentiation** labels who is talking — useful in seminars and discussion-based classes.

You can also use the **desktop app** to capture audio from any source — if your lecture is on a video call or a recorded webinar, the desktop app transcribes it directly from your computer's audio.

### After the Lecture

Every session is saved to your **Documents**. Open any transcript in the editor and:

- **Play back the audio** synced to the text — click any word and hear that exact moment
- **Word-level timestamps** let you jump to the part where the professor explained that formula
- **Translate** the transcript into your native language or any of 70+ others
- **Share** the transcript with classmates

### Study Groups

Start a broadcast session during your study group. Everyone in the group can follow along in their preferred language on their phone. The transcript is saved for everyone to review later.
